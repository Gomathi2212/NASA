Olive Ridley Sea Turtle

The Olive Ridley Sea Turtle (Lepidochelys olivacea) is a species of sea turtle known for its olive-colored shell. They are found in tropical and subtropical waters and are known for their synchronized nesting events known as arribadas.

Preventive Measures

  • Beach Conservation: Support efforts to conserve and protect nesting beaches where Olive Ridley Sea Turtles lay their eggs.
  • Light Pollution Reduction: Minimize artificial lighting on beaches during nesting seasons to prevent disorientation of hatchlings.
  • Marine Debris Cleanup: Participate in or support marine debris cleanup initiatives to prevent ingestion of plastic and other pollutants by sea turtles.
  • Responsible Fishing Practices: Advocate for and practice responsible fishing practices to reduce bycatch and protect sea turtles.
  • Educational Programs: Support educational programs and awareness campaigns that promote sea turtle conservation.
