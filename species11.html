Leatherback Sea Turtle

The Leatherback Sea Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea) is the largest species of sea turtle and is known for its distinct leathery shell. They are found in oceans around the world and are known for their long migrations and deep-diving capabilities.

Preventive Measures

  • Protection of Nesting Beaches: Advocate for the protection of nesting beaches where Leatherback Sea Turtles lay their eggs.
  • Reduction of Artificial Lighting: Minimize artificial lighting on nesting beaches during the nesting season to prevent hatchling disorientation.
  • Marine Debris Cleanup: Participate in marine debris cleanup efforts to remove plastics and other pollutants from the oceans, which pose a threat to sea turtles.
  • Responsible Fishing Practices: Promote responsible fishing practices to reduce bycatch and protect Leatherback Sea Turtles.
  • Research and Conservation: Support scientific research and conservation initiatives aimed at preserving Leatherback Sea Turtle populations.
