North Atlantic Right Whale

The North Atlantic Right Whale (Eubalaena glacialis) is a critically endangered species of whale known for its massive size and callosities on its head. They are primarily found in the North Atlantic Ocean and are known for their slow-moving behavior.

Preventive Measures

  • Marine Protected Areas: Advocate for the establishment and protection of marine protected areas to provide safe habitats for North Atlantic Right Whales.
  • Reduced Ship Strikes: Support efforts to reduce ship strikes by implementing speed limits and route adjustments in areas frequented by Right Whales.
  • Fishing Gear Modifications: Promote the use of whale-safe fishing gear and practices to minimize entanglements.
  • Acoustic Monitoring: Use underwater acoustic monitoring to track Right Whale movements and detect their presence to avoid collisions.
  • Research and Conservation: Support scientific research and conservation efforts to better understand and protect North Atlantic Right Whale populations.
