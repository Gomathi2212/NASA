Sawfish

Sawfish, also known as carpenter sharks, are a group of elasmobranch fish known for their long, flat snouts edged with sharp teeth. They are found in warm coastal waters and estuaries around the world.

Preventive Measures

  • Habitat Protection: Advocate for the protection and restoration of the coastal habitats and estuaries where Sawfish live and breed.
  • Bycatch Reduction: Support and promote fishing practices that reduce bycatch, as Sawfish are often unintentionally caught in fishing gear.
  • Regulation Enforcement: Advocate for and adhere to regulations that prohibit the capture and trade of endangered Sawfish species.
  • Educational Campaigns: Raise awareness about the importance of Sawfish conservation through educational programs and campaigns.
  • Research and Monitoring: Support scientific research and monitoring efforts to better understand Sawfish populations and their conservation needs.
