Indo-Pacific Finless Porpoise

The Indo-Pacific Finless Porpoise (Neophocaena phocaenoides) is a species of porpoise known for its lack of dorsal fin. They are found in the coastal waters of the Indian and Pacific Oceans and are known for their playful and social behavior.

Preventive Measures

  • Habitat Protection: Advocate for the protection of coastal habitats and estuaries where Indo-Pacific Finless Porpoises reside.
  • Reduced Noise Pollution: Support efforts to reduce underwater noise pollution, which can disrupt the communication and behavior of porpoises.
  • Marine Debris Cleanup: Participate in marine debris cleanup initiatives to remove plastic and other pollutants from the oceans.
  • Responsible Boating: Practice responsible boating and vessel navigation to avoid collisions with porpoises.
  • Conservation Research: Support scientific research and conservation programs aimed at understanding and protecting Indo-Pacific Finless Porpoise populations.
