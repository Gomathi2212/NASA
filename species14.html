Green Sea Turtle

The Green Sea Turtle (Chelonia mydas) is a species of sea turtle known for its greenish-colored fat and for being one of the largest sea turtles. They are found in warm coastal waters around the world and are known for their herbivorous diet.

Preventive Measures

  • Nesting Beach Protection: Advocate for the protection and preservation of nesting beaches where Green Sea Turtles lay their eggs.
  • Marine Debris Cleanup: Participate in marine debris cleanup efforts to remove plastics and other pollutants from the oceans, which pose a threat to sea turtles.
  • Responsible Boating: Practice responsible boating and vessel navigation to avoid collisions with sea turtles.
  • Conservation Education: Raise awareness about Green Sea Turtles and their conservation needs through educational programs and campaigns.
  • Sustainable Fishing Practices: Promote sustainable fishing practices to reduce bycatch and protect sea turtles.
